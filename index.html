They'll outgrow it. That's the whole idea.

The problem

Winter is expensive. Growing is free.

Every fall, families buy a full set of gear that fits for exactly one season. Every spring, most of it is still in great shape — and already too small.

For families who can't absorb that cost, the result isn't abstract. Kids stay inside at recess. They get cold walking to school. They miss the skating trip. Meanwhile, closets and landfills fill up with jackets that have one good winter left in them.

Outgrow closes that loop: the gear that's too small for one child is exactly the right size for another.

$0 → 1 season

How it works

Step one · Borrow

Pick the gear, pick the size.

Browse the library, choose what your child needs this season — snowsuit, boots, rain shell, skates — and pay a small seasonal fee based on the item's value.

Step two · Use

They wear it like they own it.

Puddles, toboggan hills, snowball diplomacy — it's all fair game. This is good gear, built to be worn hard. Normal wear is expected and covered.

Step three · Return

Outgrown? Bring it back.

When the sleeves get short or the season ends, drop the gear at any affiliate location near you. No packaging, no shipping labels, no guilt.

Step four · Clean & re-lend

We freshen it. It goes again.

Local partner cleaners inspect and professionally clean every piece, then it goes straight back into the library — right-sized for the next kid in line.

Pricing

Pay a sliver, not the sticker.

Every item rents for a season at a small percentage of what it's worth — about 12%. A $150 snowsuit becomes $18 for the whole winter. No deposits, no late-night resale scrolling, no guessing next year's size.

Families referred through schools and community agencies rent at an assisted rate — same gear, same warmth, 40% less.

Normal wear and tear is always covered. If it can be repaired, we repair it — that's the point of the loop.

Donate gear

Got a closet full of last winter?

Outgrown snowsuits, barely-worn boots, the rain jacket that fit for one spring — drop them at any affiliate location and they join the library. Brands can donate too: overstock and samples become a season of warmth instead of a write-off. Every donation is cleaned, checked, and matched to a kid who needs it.

Questions, answered

The stuff parents actually ask.

Spotless. Every returned item is professionally cleaned and inspected by our affiliate partners — real cleaners with real equipment — before it goes back into the library. If a piece doesn't pass inspection, it's repaired or retired. You'd never know it had a previous kid.

Kids grow on their own schedule, so mid-season size swaps are free. Bring the item to any affiliate location and exchange it for the next size up. We'd rather swap a jacket than see a kid in short sleeves in January.

Normal wear — scuffs, worn cuffs, the occasional zipper battle — is expected and fully covered. Kids are supposed to use this gear hard. For damage beyond repair, we'll talk it through; there are no surprise fees, and the assisted-rate program never charges damage costs.

About 12% of an item's value per season. A $150 snowsuit is $18 for the winter; a $60 pair of rain boots is about $7 for the spring. Families referred through schools and agencies pay 40% less. Try the calculator above with the exact items you need.

Affiliates clean returned gear and hand items to families, and they earn a commission on every rental that moves through their location. There's no inventory to buy — gear is donated into the library — so it's recurring revenue plus regular foot traffic, with no stock risk.
